# Special Professionals 2024

## Overview
- An averaging scheme applies to prevent eligible individuals from being pushed into a higher tax bracket due to fluctuations in income. 
- The scheme is designed for natural persons whose income from eligible sources varies significantly from year to year.

## Classes of Special Professionals
- Defined under s.405-25, the following classes are eligible for the averaging scheme:
  - Sportspersons
  - Performers
  - Writers, composers, authors
  - Art directors, costume designers, choreographers, and film editors
  - Inventors

## Exclusions from Averaging
- Under s.405-30, the following activities do not qualify a taxpayer as a special professional for the purposes of the averaging scheme:
  - Coaching or training athletes
  - Umpiring or refereeing
  - Being a member of a motor sport pit crew
  - Owning or training animals
  - Sports administration
  - Theatrical or sports entrepreneurship

## Assessable Professional Income
- Defined under s.405-20 as:
  - Income derived in relation to activities as a special professional.
  - Prizes and rewards for activities as a special professional.
  - Fees from endorsements, interviews, commentary, advertisements, and promotions relating to special professional activities.

## Conditional Availability
- A taxpayer is not classified as a special professional by merely engaging in a listed profession under s.405-30(1) if there is substantial continuity in providing services to another person.
